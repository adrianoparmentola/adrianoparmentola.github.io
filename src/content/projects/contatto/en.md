(con)tatto is a community-based service that helps over-75 residents in Pianoro
age better by combining prevention, social connection, proximity networks, and
light support systems.

Pianoro has 17,866 residents; the over-75 population is around 2,703 people
(15.13%), and the nearest emergency service is around 17.5 minutes away. Many
older residents live alone or with a weak family network. The design question:
how can design support prevention, autonomy, and social connection while
reducing isolation — without replacing healthcare institutions?

## Three levels

**Connessione** — built around Centro Giusti as a *Third Place of Longevity*:
shared grocery shopping, gentle gymnastics, a monthly community newspaper,
skill-sharing (a local Repair Café), coordinated through an analog board
(the *Bacheca*) so the service stays accessible without requiring digital tools.

**Tocco** — proximity circles (*Cerchio di Prossimità*) of up to ~20 residents
within short walking distance, built to create weak ties and mutual awareness,
piloted as *Cerchio 0* around Bar Valentina.

**Delicatezza** — the most discreet layer: *Linea Delicata*, a volunteer-run
phone line connected to Centro Giusti offering company, listening, information,
and activation of the local volunteer network.
