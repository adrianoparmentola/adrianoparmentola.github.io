Linea Dotta proposes an alternative to road-based freight logistics: goods move
through a modular underground network connected to surface-level logistics hubs,
aimed at reducing road congestion, emissions, and the safety risks of large
delivery vehicles in dense urban areas.

The system is composed of underground transport channels, modular tubes,
sensorized wagons, magnetic-levitation rails, electric propulsion, energy
recovery, and urban logistics hubs — located in Bologna, using the relationship
between historical canal routes and future logistics scenarios.

## Method

The project follows a **Design for Six Sigma (DFSS)** logic through the
**DMADV** sequence, supported by **QFD**, **Kano analysis**, and **TRIZ**:

- **Benchmark** of existing logistics operators to define performance parameters.
- **QFD** (Cosa/Come matrix) translating needs into technical requirements:
  reliability, maintenance frequency, safety, environmental impact, speed, cost.
- **Kano analysis** classifying requirements as basic, performance, or
  satisfaction attributes.
- **TRIZ** resolving contradictions — speed vs. energy consumption,
  accessibility vs. fabrication complexity, reliability vs. repairability —
  into design principles: modularity, segmentation, magnetic levitation,
  energy recovery, predictive maintenance.
- **Verify** through SWOT analysis, style analysis, and 3D visualization.
